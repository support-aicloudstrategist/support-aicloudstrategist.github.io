DPDP compliance for Indian diagnostic labs

Patient trust is becoming a business requirement. AICS helps diagnostic labs put practical website, consent and data-handling basics in place in 2 weeks.

Indian healthcare businesses handle sensitive personal data through websites, WhatsApp, forms, spreadsheets and vendors. A diagnostic lab collects names, phone numbers, appointment or test details, payment context and sometimes family information.

Full enforcement is expected around May 13, 2027, and penalties can go up to ₹250 crore for serious failures. The practical goal is to install working controls before customers, partners or regulators ask for evidence.

Why diagnostic labs are exposed under DPDP

Data moves through reception, doctors or technicians, external vendors, website forms, WhatsApp Business, email and payment tools. If there is no written privacy notice, consent language, data inventory or patient-rights process, the business cannot answer basic questions confidently.

The 8 things every diagnostic lab website needs

  • Privacy policy customized for the business.
  • Cookie banner where cookies or analytics are used.
  • Consent flow for forms and WhatsApp follow-up.
  • Data inventory listing storage, owners and vendors.
  • Breach response plan.
  • Retention policy.
  • Vendor register or agreements.
  • Patient rights process.
