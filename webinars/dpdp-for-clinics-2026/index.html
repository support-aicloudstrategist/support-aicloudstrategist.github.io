Why this matters

India's DPDP Act moves privacy from a back-office topic to an operating requirement. By November 2026, clinics handling patient enquiries, appointment data, reports, WhatsApp conversations and follow-up lists need cleaner consent, notice, retention and handling practices.

Penalty exposure can be serious, but the bigger practical risk is trust. Patients are already sharing sensitive information. A clinic that can explain what it collects, why, and how it handles that data will look more professional than one waiting until the last month.
