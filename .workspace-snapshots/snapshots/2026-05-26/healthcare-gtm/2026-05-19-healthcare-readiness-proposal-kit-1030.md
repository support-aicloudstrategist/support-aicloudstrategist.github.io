## 2) Proposal / SOW Insert

### Proposal Title

Healthcare Digital Readiness Audit for `[Client Name]`

### Executive Summary

AICloudStrategist proposes a focused healthcare digital readiness audit for `[Client Name]`. The engagement will identify practical operational gaps in patient enquiry handling, appointment workflows, billing/report delivery, backups, access hygiene, and management visibility.

The objective is to produce a concise scorecard and 30-day improvement roadmap that helps leadership decide the smallest useful next step — without committing to a full HIS/EMR/LIS replacement or major technology migration upfront.

### Business Context

Healthcare teams often operate with a mix of phone calls, WhatsApp, walk-ins, front-desk notes, billing systems, report tools, spreadsheets, and vendor software. These systems may work individually, but gaps appear when enquiries, appointments, reports, follow-ups, and backups are not visible end-to-end.

Typical risks include:

- missed enquiries or delayed callbacks
- manual follow-ups with no ageing queue
- poor visibility into pending reports, no-shows, or repeat-visit reminders
- backups that exist but are not tested
- unclear staff access and device hygiene
- leadership lacking a daily operational dashboard

### Objectives

1. Map the current enquiry-to-appointment-to-report workflow.
2. Score practical readiness across patient communication, operations, resilience, and visibility.
3. Identify the top 2–3 improvement actions that can be started within 30 days.
4. Separate quick operational fixes from larger system or integration decisions.
5. Provide leadership with a simple implementation estimate for the next phase.

### Scope of Work

| Workstream | Activities | Deliverables |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery | 30–45 minute leadership/admin discussion; clarify locations, systems, pain points, and priorities | Discovery notes and current-state summary |
| Workflow mapping | Map enquiry → appointment → billing → consultation/test → report/prescription → follow-up | Simple workflow map |
| Scorecard assessment | Review 10 readiness areas: enquiries, appointments, billing, reports, follow-up, integration, backups, access, website/trust, visibility | Readiness scorecard with gap notes |
| Risk and leakage review | Identify operational leakage, backup/access risks, and visibility gaps | Prioritised findings register |
| 30-day roadmap | Rank fixes by impact, effort, urgency, and dependency | 30-day action plan |
| Handover | Walkthrough with leadership/admin team | Final review call and next-step recommendation |

### Out of Scope Unless Separately Agreed

- Medical, legal, regulatory, DPDP, NABH, ABDM, or cybersecurity certification
- Production changes to HIS/EMR/LIS/billing/lab/report systems
- Patient-data migration or custom software development
- Emergency support or 24x7 managed service
- Public claims about patient outcomes or client results
- Third-party software purchases without separate approval

### Deliverables

| Deliverable | Format | Acceptance Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| Current workflow map | PDF/Markdown/diagram | Shows major enquiry, appointment, billing/report, and follow-up touchpoints |

| 30-day improvement roadmap | PDF/Markdown | Separates quick wins, medium-effort fixes, and optional implementation work |

## 3) Discovery Call Guide

### Opening

> Thanks for taking the time. The purpose of this call is to understand your current digital workflow — from patient enquiry to appointment, billing, reports, follow-up, backup, and management visibility. This is not a software replacement pitch and not a compliance certification. The goal is to identify the top practical improvements that would help in the next 30 days.

### Qualifying questions

1. How many doctors, staff members, daily patients, and locations are active today?
2. What systems are used for appointments, billing, EMR/HIS/LIS, accounting, reports, and WhatsApp/email/SMS?
3. Where do most patient enquiries come from: Google, website, calls, WhatsApp, walk-ins, referrals, or campaigns?
4. How are missed calls, missed WhatsApp messages, no-shows, and pending follow-ups tracked?
5. Which process still depends on paper, Excel, phone calls, or staff memory?
6. How are reports/prescriptions delivered, and how are delays or pending items monitored?
7. How often are backups taken, where are they stored, and when was the last restore test?
8. Who can access patient records, reports, billing, and admin dashboards?
9. What dashboard would leadership want to see daily?
10. Which one improvement would create the highest value in the next 30 days?

### Close

> Based on what you shared, the next step would be a focused readiness audit. We map the current workflow, score the main risk/leakage areas, and give you a 30-day improvement roadmap. If useful, I can share a short proposal with scope, timeline, and fee range for leadership review.
