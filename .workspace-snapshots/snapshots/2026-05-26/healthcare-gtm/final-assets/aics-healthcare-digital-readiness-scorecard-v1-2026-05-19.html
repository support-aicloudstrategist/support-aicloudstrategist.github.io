Healthcare Digital Readiness Audit

Is your clinic, lab, or hospital losing time because digital systems are not connected?

Many healthcare businesses already use software for billing, reports, appointments, accounting, or patient communication. The operational gap usually appears between those systems: missed enquiries, delayed reports, manual follow-ups, weak backup routines, limited dashboard visibility, and unclear ownership when something breaks.

AICloudStrategist’s Healthcare Digital Readiness Scorecard helps leadership identify practical technology and workflow gaps without forcing a full system replacement.

Best fit

  • Specialty clinics and multi-doctor practices
  • Diagnostic centres and pathology labs
  • Small hospitals and nursing homes
  • Healthcare businesses planning HIS, EMR, LIS, cloud, backup, automation, or digital presence upgrades

  4. 30-day improvement roadmap
  5. Implementation estimate

Score each area from 1 to 5

1 = manual/high risk · 3 = functional but inconsistent · 5 = reliable, measurable, and scalable

Readiness areaScore

Appointment workflow — booking, reminders, rescheduling, queue visibility, missed follow-ups/5
Billing and payment flow — billing accuracy, payment capture, GST/TDS/claims coordination if relevant/5
Reports/prescriptions delivery — turnaround time, WhatsApp/email/SMS delivery, patient access experience/5

Readiness interpretation

0–20: Critical gapsImmediate fixes needed for reliability, backup, and patient communication.
21–35: Operational but leakySystems work, but manual gaps create delays, revenue leakage, or admin load.
36–45: Growth-ready foundationGood base; focus on automation, analytics, security hardening, and patient experience.
46–50: Scale-readyOptimise integrations, dashboards, compliance evidence, and multi-location governance.

Discovery questions for leadership

  1. Which systems are used today for appointments, billing, EMR/HIS/LIS, accounting, and reports?
  2. Where do most patient enquiries come from: Google, website, WhatsApp, referral, walk-in, or campaigns?
  3. How are missed calls, missed WhatsApp enquiries, no-shows, and pending reports tracked?
  4. What still depends on paper, Excel, phone calls, or staff memory?
  5. How often are backups taken, and when was the last successful restore test?
  6. Who can access patient records, reports, billing, and admin dashboards?
  7. What happens if the front-desk PC, local server, internet, or main software stops working for one day?
  8. Which improvement would create the highest business value in the next 30 days?
