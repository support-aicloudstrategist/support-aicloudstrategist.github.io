## Publish-ready FAQ copy

### Frequently asked questions

#### Is this a software replacement project?

No. The first step is a practical readiness review. We map how enquiries, appointments, billing, reports, follow-ups, backups, and visibility work today, then identify the smallest useful improvements. If a software change, integration, dashboard, backup hardening, or automation project is useful later, it is proposed separately.

#### Do we need to share patient data?

Usually no. The review can be done with process walkthroughs, non-sensitive screenshots, sample formats, and team inputs. If any sensitive data is ever needed, it must be minimised, approved, and handled separately.

#### Is this a medical, legal, DPDP, NABH, ABDM, or cybersecurity certification?

No. This is not a medical, legal, regulatory, DPDP, NABH, ABDM, or cybersecurity certification. It is a practical technology and workflow readiness review for business improvement, operational resilience, and better digital execution.

#### What does the scorecard cover?

The scorecard reviews 10 practical areas: patient enquiry capture, appointment workflow, billing and payment flow, report/prescription delivery, follow-up and repeat visits, system integration, backup and disaster recovery, access and data hygiene, website and trust presence, and management visibility.

#### Who should attend the discovery call?

Ideally one owner/leader, one admin or operations person, and one person who understands the current systems. For smaller clinics, the owner and front-desk/admin lead are enough.

#### How long does the audit take?

Most audits take 5–8 working days after discovery, depending on number of locations, systems, and inputs available.

#### What do we receive at the end?

You receive a workflow map, readiness scorecard, risk/leakage register, 30-day improvement roadmap, and a non-binding implementation estimate for optional next steps.

#### What is the fee range?

The Healthcare Digital Readiness Audit is typically ₹15,000–₹35,000 + applicable taxes, depending on organisation size, number of locations, and number of systems reviewed.

## Objection-handling copy for page or sales enablement

### “We already have billing / HIS / lab software.”

That is common. The review does not assume your existing software is wrong. We look at the gaps between systems: missed enquiries, manual follow-ups, no-show tracking, pending report visibility, backups, access hygiene, and management dashboards.

### “We are too small for an audit.”

Small teams often benefit most because missed calls, WhatsApp follow-ups, paper notes, Excel sheets, and staff-memory dependencies can quietly create leakage. The scorecard is designed to find practical fixes, not create a large IT project.

### “We do not want disruption.”

The review is non-disruptive. It starts with discussions, workflow mapping, and safe process evidence. Production system changes are out of scope unless separately approved.

### “We are worried about sensitive data.”

The audit should avoid patient data wherever possible. We focus on process, workflow, controls, and visibility. Any sensitive-data handling must be minimised and separately approved.
