## Recommended FAQ block

### Frequently asked questions

#### Is this a software replacement project?
No. The first step is a focused readiness audit. We review how enquiries, appointments, billing/report delivery, follow-ups, backups, access and leadership visibility work today. If software changes are useful, they are proposed separately after the scorecard and roadmap.

#### Do you need patient data to run the audit?
No patient data is needed for the initial readiness review. We prefer process walkthroughs, system names, anonymised examples, screenshots with sensitive details hidden, and leadership/admin inputs. Any sensitive-data handling must be agreed separately.

#### Is this a DPDP, NABH, ABDM, legal, medical or cybersecurity certification?
No. This is a practical technology and workflow readiness review for business operations. It can highlight gaps and next steps, but it does not certify legal, medical, DPDP, NABH, ABDM or cybersecurity compliance.

#### What will we receive at the end?
You receive a current workflow map, a 10-area Healthcare Digital Readiness Scorecard, a practical risk/leakage register, a 30-day improvement roadmap, and an optional implementation estimate.

#### How long does it take?
Most reviews take 5–8 working days, depending on number of locations, systems, team availability and clarity of current workflows.

#### Who should join the first 20-minute readiness review?
A founder, owner, administrator, operations manager or clinic/lab/hospital leader who understands enquiries, appointments, billing/report flow, patient communication and current operational bottlenecks.

#### What if we already use HIS, EMR, LIS or billing software?
That is normal. The audit does not assume your existing software is wrong. We look for gaps around the software: enquiry capture, staff handoffs, reminders, report delivery, backups, access hygiene, dashboards and follow-up visibility.

## Objection-handling microcopy for landing page

**“We are not ready for a big IT project.”**  
That is exactly why the audit starts small. The output is a practical 30-day roadmap, not a forced replacement or large migration.

**“Our team is already busy.”**  
The review is designed to minimise disruption: one leadership/admin discovery conversation, targeted workflow inputs, and a concise walkthrough of findings.

**“We are worried about data privacy.”**  
The initial review avoids patient data by default and uses anonymised/process-level information wherever possible. Any deeper access requires separate approval.
