Where leakage happens

Most healthcare businesses do not lose enquiries in one place. They lose them in the handoff.

Missed first response

A patient enquiry comes through WhatsApp, Google Business Profile, website form, Instagram, or a phone call. If nobody owns the first response window, the lead quietly goes cold.

No visible follow-up status

The team remembers some callbacks, writes some notes, and leaves some enquiries inside chat history. The owner cannot see which leads are new, contacted, waiting, booked, or lost.

Weak trust signals before booking

Patients want clear location, service pages, timings, enquiry paths, privacy comfort, and a professional digital experience. If the website or profile feels incomplete, they compare alternatives.

Example pattern

A typical leakage pattern we look for.

Illustrative workflow example, not a client case study.

Before

  • Enquiries arrive through WhatsApp, calls, and social DMs.
  • Reception/team members reply manually with no shared status view.
  • The owner sees activity but not conversion quality.
  • Follow-ups depend on memory or scattered chat history.
  • Website and profile CTAs do not clearly route people to the same review path.

After the recommended sprint

  • Every enquiry gets a source, status, next action, and owner.
  • WhatsApp and call follow-ups use consistent templates.
  • The website points visitors to one clear free review/booking path.
  • The owner can review pending callbacks and ageing leads daily.
  • Privacy-sensitive information is kept out of public forms and early review conversations.
