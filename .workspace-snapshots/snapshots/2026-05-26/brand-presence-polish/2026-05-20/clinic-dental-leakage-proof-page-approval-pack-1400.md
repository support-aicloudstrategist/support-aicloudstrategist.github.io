## Problem section

**Section tag:** Where leakage happens  
**H2:** Most healthcare businesses do not lose enquiries in one place. They lose them in the handoff.

**Cards:**

1. **Missed first response**  
   A patient enquiry comes through WhatsApp, Google Business Profile, website form, Instagram, or a phone call. If nobody owns the first response window, the lead quietly goes cold.

2. **No visible follow-up status**  
   The team remembers some callbacks, writes some notes, and leaves some enquiries inside chat history. The owner cannot see which leads are new, contacted, waiting, booked, or lost.

3. **Weak trust signals before booking**  
   Patients want clear location, service pages, timings, enquiry paths, privacy comfort, and a professional digital experience. If the website or profile feels incomplete, they compare alternatives.

## Review framework

**Section tag:** What AICS checks  
**H2:** The review maps your enquiry journey, not just your website.

**Checklist:**

- Website or landing page clarity: services, location, timings, CTA, mobile usability, forms, and trust blocks.
- WhatsApp journey: welcome message, response ownership, labels/statuses, callback date, and repeatable templates.
- Call handling: missed-call capture, callback process, owner visibility, and appointment handoff.
- Google/social presence: consistent contact details, local trust signals, and enquiry routes.
- Privacy and trust basics: safe public language, consent comfort, and no unnecessary sensitive-data collection.
- Owner dashboard readiness: simple view of new, contacted, booked, pending, and lost enquiries.

## Proof-style scenario

**Section tag:** Example pattern  
**H2:** A typical leakage pattern we look for.

**Important label:** Illustrative workflow example, not a client case study.

**Before:**

- Enquiries arrive through WhatsApp, calls, and social DMs.
- Reception/team members reply manually with no shared status view.
- The owner sees activity but not conversion quality.
- Follow-ups depend on memory or scattered chat history.
- Website and profile CTAs do not clearly route people to the same review path.

**After the recommended sprint:**

- Every enquiry gets a source, status, next action, and owner.
- WhatsApp and call follow-ups use consistent templates.
- The website points visitors to one clear free review/booking path.
- The owner can review pending callbacks and ageing leads daily.
- Privacy-sensitive information is kept out of public forms and early review conversations.

**Business outcome:** Fewer silent drop-offs, clearer team ownership, better patient response discipline, and a practical base for automation later.
