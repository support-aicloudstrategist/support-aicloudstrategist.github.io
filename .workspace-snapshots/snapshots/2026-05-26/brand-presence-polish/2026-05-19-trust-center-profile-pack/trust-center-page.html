What this Trust Center covers

This page explains the standards we follow across AICloudStrategist Local and AICloudStrategist Advisory.

  • How we handle business information shared during reviews and projects.
  • How we approach privacy notices, DPDP readiness, and consent basics.
  • How we use AI-assisted operations responsibly.
  • How we set scope, pricing, timelines, and delivery boundaries.
  • How clients can contact us about privacy, correction, or service concerns.

1. Clear scope before delivery

Every paid sprint or advisory engagement should start with a written scope covering the business problem, deliverables, timeline, required access, exclusions, review process, and handover method.

We avoid vague transformation language. If a business needs a website, lead flow, CRM-lite tracker, cloud review, cost optimisation plan, or compliance-readiness checklist, the deliverable should be named clearly before work begins.

2. Privacy and DPDP readiness mindset

AICloudStrategist is not a law firm and does not provide legal certification. Our role is to help businesses create practical privacy and DPDP-readiness foundations that can be reviewed by legal or compliance advisors when required.

  • Website privacy notice structure.
  • Consent and contact-form review.
  • Vendor and tool register template.
  • Personal data inventory starter sheet.
  • Breach-readiness checklist.
  • Owner-friendly remediation roadmap.

We do not claim that a business is fully compliant merely because a template or checklist has been delivered. Compliance depends on actual business practices, legal review, implementation, monitoring, and staff behaviour.

3. Careful handling of client information

During a review or project, a client may share business details such as website links, process screenshots, cloud cost exports, workflow notes, vendor names, or contact-flow examples.

  • Request only the information needed for the agreed work.
  • Avoid collecting unnecessary personal or sensitive data.
  • Use shared information only for the requested review or delivery.
  • Avoid publishing client-identifiable material without approval.
  • Recommend removal or masking of sensitive information before screenshots, case studies, or public proof assets are used.

If a project requires access to production systems, cloud consoles, DNS, analytics, business email, CRM tools, or other sensitive systems, access terms should be agreed separately before work begins.

4. AI-assisted delivery with human approval gates

AICloudStrategist uses AI-assisted workflows to speed up research, documentation, analysis, content drafting, and operational preparation. AI helps us work faster, but public posting, client outreach, paid actions, external communications, and sensitive access decisions require human approval.

  • No public publishing without approval.
  • No client outreach without approval of the exact message.
  • No invented proof, testimonials, client names, or guarantees.
  • No legal, financial, or compliance certification claims without qualified review.
  • No unnecessary exposure of private business or personal information.

5. Security-first digital foundations

For local business and SMB work, security basics often matter more than complex tools. We focus on foundations that reduce avoidable risk.

  • Clear ownership of website domain, hosting, email, and analytics.
  • Secure contact forms and lead-routing paths.
  • HTTPS and basic site hygiene checks.
  • Privacy and consent wording appropriate to the business context.
  • Backup and admin-access awareness.
  • Avoiding public exposure of private phone numbers, internal documents, or credentials.

For advisory work, the security scope may include cloud identity, logging, backup posture, cost controls, DevSecOps practices, observability, incident-readiness, and AI/MLOps governance.

6. Honest claims and realistic outcomes

AICloudStrategist does not promise guaranteed Google rankings, fixed savings percentages, guaranteed leads, or instant compliance. We can improve digital presence, clarify workflows, reduce avoidable waste, and provide practical roadmaps, but results depend on the client’s market, implementation, budget, operations, and follow-through.

Our communication standard is to say what we know, what we have checked, and what still requires validation.

7. Public proof and case-study standards

Before publishing a case study, testimonial, screenshot, or client outcome, we should confirm client approval, remove or mask private information, validate numbers, explain the problem/action/outcome clearly, and avoid exposing confidential architecture, pricing, or operational weaknesses.

Where client permission is not available, we may publish anonymised examples or template-style assets that explain the method without identifying the business.
