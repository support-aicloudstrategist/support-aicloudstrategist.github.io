What this covers

Clear standards before bigger digital work starts.

This page explains the standards we follow across AICloudStrategist Local and AICloudStrategist Advisory.

Information handling

How business information is requested, used, masked, and handed back during reviews and delivery.

Privacy and DPDP readiness

Practical privacy notices, consent basics, registers, checklists, and roadmaps that can be reviewed by qualified advisors where required.

AI-assisted operations

AI may help with research and drafting, but client outreach, public publishing, paid actions, and sensitive access decisions require approval.

Scope, pricing, and delivery boundaries

Every engagement should have named deliverables, timelines, assumptions, review steps, and honest limits.

1. Clear scope before delivery

Every paid sprint or advisory engagement should start with a written scope.

The scope should explain the business problem, exact deliverables, timeline, information or access required, exclusions, review process, and handover. We avoid vague transformation language and name the actual outcome: website, lead flow, CRM-lite tracker, cloud review, cost optimisation plan, compliance-readiness checklist, or another specific deliverable.

2. Privacy and DPDP readiness mindset

Practical readiness, not legal certification.

AICloudStrategist is not a law firm and does not provide legal certification. We help businesses create practical privacy and DPDP-readiness foundations that can be reviewed by legal or compliance advisors when required.

  • Website privacy notice structure
  • Consent and contact-form review
  • Vendor and tool register template
  • Personal data inventory starter sheet
  • Breach-readiness checklist
  • Owner-friendly remediation roadmap
3. Careful handling of client information

We request only what is needed for the agreed work.

During a review or project, a client may share website links, process screenshots, cloud cost exports, workflow notes, vendor names, or contact-flow examples. Our standard is to avoid unnecessary personal or sensitive data, use shared information only for the requested delivery, and remove or mask sensitive information before screenshots, case studies, or public proof assets are used.

If production systems, cloud consoles, DNS, analytics, business email, CRM tools, or other sensitive systems are required, access terms should be agreed separately before work begins.

4. AI-assisted delivery with human approval gates

AI helps speed up preparation; approvals protect the client and the business.

  • No public publishing without approval.
  • No client outreach without approval of the exact message.
  • No invented proof, testimonials, client names, or guarantees.
  • No legal, financial, or compliance certification claims without qualified review.
  • No unnecessary exposure of private business or personal information.
  • Client-facing documents should be readable, structured, and reviewed before sharing.
5. Security-first digital foundations

For SMB work, the basics often matter most.

We focus on foundations that reduce avoidable risk: ownership of domains, hosting, email and analytics; secure contact forms and lead routing; HTTPS and site hygiene; privacy and consent wording; backup and admin-access awareness; and avoiding public exposure of private phone numbers, internal documents, or credentials.

For advisory work, scope may include cloud identity, logging, backup posture, cost controls, DevSecOps practices, observability, incident-readiness, and AI/MLOps governance.

6. Honest claims and realistic outcomes

No fake certainty.

AICloudStrategist does not promise guaranteed Google rankings, fixed savings percentages, guaranteed leads, or instant compliance. We can improve digital presence, clarify workflows, reduce avoidable waste, and provide practical roadmaps, but results depend on the client’s market, implementation, budget, operations, and follow-through.

7. Public proof and case-study standards

Proof must be approved, accurate, and responsible.

Before publishing a case study, testimonial, screenshot, or client outcome, we confirm the client has approved public use, private information has been removed or masked, numbers are accurate, and the story does not expose confidential architecture, pricing, or operational weaknesses.
