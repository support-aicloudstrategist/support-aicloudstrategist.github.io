## Day 1 — suggested Mon 10:00 IST

Lost leads are usually process leaks.

Most clinics do not lose enquiries because patients are not interested.

They lose enquiries because the journey is broken:
- website CTA unclear
- WhatsApp reply delayed
- missed calls not logged
- forms do not confirm next steps
- follow-up depends on memory

Before buying more ads, inspect the path from Google search → website → WhatsApp/call/form → staff follow-up → booking.

That is where the real leakage usually sits.

Privacy readiness is a trust signal.

For small healthcare businesses, privacy readiness is not only a compliance topic.

It is a trust signal.

Simple first steps:
- visible privacy note
- consent/helper text near forms
- clear WhatsApp enquiry handling
- staff access discipline
- vendor/tool register
- retention basics

## Day 5 — suggested Fri 11:30 IST

Before paid ads, inspect leakage.

Before a local business spends more on paid ads, it should answer five questions:

1. Does the landing page explain the offer clearly?
2. Is WhatsApp/call visible on mobile?
3. Are missed calls tracked?

5. Does the owner see weekly enquiry outcomes?

## Day 6 — suggested Sat 10:30 IST

Proof should be earned, not invented.

Early-stage businesses face a trust challenge.

They need proof, but they should not fake proof.

No borrowed logos.
No invented testimonials.
No exaggerated ROI numbers.
No “case studies” that never happened.

A better path:
- be transparent about early-stage status
- offer founder customer terms
- document real work
- publish real outcomes only after client approval
- show process quality before proof volume exists

Trust compounds when claims stay honest.
