# Clinic Digital Trust & Automation Checklist

**15-minute self-audit for clinics, diagnostic labs, pharmacies, physio/dental practices, and home-care teams**

A practical digital safety and workflow review for healthcare MSMEs that want fewer missed appointments, safer patient data, and a more professional patient experience — without buying a heavy hospital IT system.

**Use this checklist to spot leakage in seven areas:**

| Area | Quick self-check | Risk if ignored | Fast improvement idea |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Enquiries and appointments | Are website forms, calls, and WhatsApp enquiries assigned to a person with a same-day follow-up trail? | Missed bookings, duplicate work, angry patients. | One intake board with owner-visible status: New → Called → Booked → Follow-up. |
| 2. Patient data storage | Is patient/report data stored in controlled systems instead of personal phones, loose Excel files, or shared inboxes? | Data loss, privacy exposure, staff dependency. | Centralise records with role-based access and basic audit history. |
| 3. Report delivery | Can staff see report status and notify patients without searching multiple chats/files? | Repeated calls, delayed reports, poor trust. | Create a report-ready notification workflow and standard response scripts. |
| 4. Backup and recovery | Are critical reports, billing files, website leads, and operational sheets backed up automatically? | One laptop/phone failure can stop operations. | Daily cloud backup with monthly restore test. |
| 5. WhatsApp, phone, and website trust | Do your website, Google profile, WhatsApp, and phone line show consistent current contact details? | Patients hesitate or contact the wrong channel. | Standardise website/profile CTA and route WhatsApp requests to one owner-visible queue. |
| 6. Staff access and audit trail | Do staff have only the access they need, with MFA/password discipline? | Ex-staff or shared passwords can expose sensitive data. | Remove shared passwords; enable MFA; review access monthly. |
| 7. Growth automation | Are follow-ups, reminders, feedback, repeat visits, and review requests manually dependent on memory? | Revenue leakage and inconsistent patient experience. | Automate reminders and post-visit feedback where consent and policy allow. |

## Score yourself

- **0–2 areas weak:** Good foundation. Prioritise backup, MFA, and enquiry visibility.
- **3–5 areas weak:** Workflow leakage is likely costing bookings and staff time. A 7-day action plan is useful.
- **6–7 areas weak:** Treat this as operational risk. Start with patient-data control, backup, and enquiry/report workflow.
