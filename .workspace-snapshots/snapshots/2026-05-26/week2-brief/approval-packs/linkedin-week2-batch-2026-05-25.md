## Day 9 — DPDP enforcement timeline — what changes Nov 2026

Suggested posting time: 11:00 AM IST

Decision sought: APPROVE / REJECT / EDIT

November 2026 is not just a date on a compliance calendar.

For clinics and labs, it means patient data handling needs to become explainable: what you collect, why you collect it, where it goes, who can access it, and how a patient can ask questions.

The risky part is not only big databases. It is the everyday flow: website forms, WhatsApp reports, appointment requests, Google forms, staff follow-ups, and old spreadsheets.

My suggestion: don’t wait for panic. Start with a simple data map and a consent check.

2. Is WhatsApp or call visible in the first screen?

Ads can bring attention. They cannot fix a broken enquiry path.

## Day 13 — 3 DPDP myths Indian SMB owners should ignore

Suggested posting time: 11:30 AM IST

Decision sought: APPROVE / REJECT / EDIT

Three DPDP myths I want clinic and SMB owners to ignore:

Myth 1: “Only large companies need to worry.”
If you collect personal data, you need basic hygiene.

Myth 2: “A privacy policy alone is enough.”
The policy must match the actual data flow.

Myth 3: “Consent is only a legal checkbox.”
Clear consent can also improve trust and enquiry quality.

You don’t need panic. You need a practical sequence: map data, fix forms, document consent, train staff.

Soft CTA: Start small. A 14-day readiness sprint is often enough to get moving.
