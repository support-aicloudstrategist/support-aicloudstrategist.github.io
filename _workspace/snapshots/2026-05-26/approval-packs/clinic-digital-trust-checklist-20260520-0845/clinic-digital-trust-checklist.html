Clinic Digital Trust & Automation Checklist

For clinics, diagnostic labs, pharmacies, physio/dental practices, and home-care teams that want fewer missed appointments, safer patient data, and a more professional patient experience — without buying a heavy hospital IT system.

1. Enquiries and appointments

Self-check: Are website forms, calls, and WhatsApp enquiries assigned to a person with a same-day follow-up trail?

Risk: Missed bookings, duplicate work, angry patients.

Fast improvement: One intake board with owner-visible status: New → Called → Booked → Follow-up.

2. Patient data storage

Self-check: Is patient/report data stored in controlled systems instead of personal phones, loose Excel files, or shared inboxes?

Risk: Data loss, privacy exposure, staff dependency.

Fast improvement: Centralise records with role-based access and basic audit history.

3. Report delivery

Self-check: Can staff see report status and notify patients without searching multiple chats/files?

Risk: Repeated calls, delayed reports, poor trust.

Fast improvement: Create a report-ready notification workflow and standard response scripts.

4. Backup and recovery

Self-check: Are critical reports, billing files, website leads, and operational sheets backed up automatically?

Risk: One laptop/phone failure can stop operations.

Fast improvement: Daily cloud backup with monthly restore test.

5. WhatsApp, phone, and website trust

Self-check: Do your website, Google profile, WhatsApp, and phone line show consistent current contact details?

Risk: Patients hesitate or contact the wrong channel.

Fast improvement: Standardise website/profile CTA and route WhatsApp requests to one owner-visible queue.

6. Staff access and audit trail

Self-check: Do staff have only the access they need, with MFA/password discipline?

Risk: Ex-staff or shared passwords can expose sensitive data.

Fast improvement: Remove shared passwords; enable MFA; review access monthly.

7. Growth automation

Self-check: Are follow-ups, reminders, feedback, repeat visits, and review requests manually dependent on memory?

Risk: Revenue leakage and inconsistent patient experience.

Fast improvement: Automate reminders and post-visit feedback where consent and policy allow.

Score yourself

  • 0–2 areas weak: Good foundation. Prioritise backup, MFA, and enquiry visibility.
  • 3–5 areas weak: Workflow leakage is likely costing bookings and staff time. A 7-day action plan is useful.
  • 6–7 areas weak: Treat this as operational risk. Start with patient-data control, backup, and enquiry/report workflow.
