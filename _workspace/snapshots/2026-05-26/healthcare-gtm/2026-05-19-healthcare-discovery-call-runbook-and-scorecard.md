## 1) 20-minute discovery structure

### Minute 0–2 — Permission and context

**Operator script:**

> Thanks for taking the time. This is a practical technology and workflow readiness review, not a medical, legal, or regulatory certification. I’ll ask a few questions about enquiries, appointments, reports, backups, security basics, and management visibility, then share the top improvement areas if useful.

**Confirm:**

- Who owns digital operations/admin follow-up?
- Which location(s) or department(s) should the review focus on?
- Is the current priority growth, leakage reduction, continuity, website trust, automation, or management reporting?

### Minute 2–7 — Patient enquiry and appointment flow

Ask:

1. Where do new patient enquiries come from today — Google Business, website, WhatsApp, calls, walk-ins, referrals, campaigns?
2. How are missed calls, WhatsApp messages, website forms, and no-shows tracked?
3. Who follows up, and what happens if the front desk is busy?
4. Do owners/admins see daily enquiry and appointment numbers?

**Signals to capture:** missed enquiry risk, manual spreadsheet/diary dependency, no source tracking, no dashboard.

### Minute 7–12 — Billing/report/prescription/follow-up handoff

Ask:

1. Which software/tools are used for billing, appointments, reports, EMR/HIS/LIS, accounting, and communication?
2. How are reports or prescriptions delivered — print, WhatsApp, email, portal, manual pickup?
3. Where do delays, repeat calls, pending reports, or follow-up gaps occur?
4. Are review requests, follow-up reminders, packages, or chronic-care check-ins tracked?

**Signals to capture:** patient communication leakage, duplicate entry, staff-memory dependencies, weak repeat-visit process.

### Minute 12–16 — Backup, access, and continuity basics

Ask:

1. How often are key systems backed up, and where is the backup stored?
2. When was the last restore test?
3. What happens if the front-desk PC/server/internet/vendor software is unavailable for a day?
4. Who can access billing, reports, patient records, admin accounts, and website/Google Business/WhatsApp tools?
5. What is the staff exit process for account/device access?

**Signals to capture:** no offsite backup, no restore test, shared passwords, no access revocation, vendor lock-in risk.

### Minute 16–20 — Score, summarize, and next step

**Operator script:**

> Based on what you shared, I’d score the highest-impact areas as: [area 1], [area 2], [area 3]. The practical next step would be a short readiness audit where we map the workflow, score risks, and give a 30-day improvement roadmap.

**If interested:**

> I can share the scope and a fixed-price audit range. It is normally ₹15,000–₹35,000 + taxes depending on systems and locations reviewed.

**If not now:**

> No problem. I can share the scorecard as a checklist for your admin team.
