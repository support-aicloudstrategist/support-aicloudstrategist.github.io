# Webinar deck summary — DPDP for Indian Clinics

Deck: `aics-dpdp-for-clinics-deck-2026-05-25.pptx`
Presenter: Anushka Bhattacharya, Director, AICloudStrategist
Audience: Indian clinic owners — dental, diagnostic, aesthetic, medical
Format: 40-45 min presentation + 15-min Q&A

## Section talking points

### Slides 1-3 — Opening
- Set expectations: practical DPDP readiness, not legal advice.
- Introduce Anushka and AICloudStrategist honestly as early-stage.
- Promise a checklist, exposure map, and 14-day action path.

### Slides 4-8 — DPDP overview
- Explain personal data in clinic language.
- November 2026 is a planning deadline, not a panic line.
- Risk includes everyday workflows, not only large databases.
- SMB clinics are exposed because data flows through forms, WhatsApp, staff devices and listings.

### Slides 9-15 — Clinic exposure points
- Review website forms, WhatsApp, reports, listings, staff phones, patient requests and privacy notice mismatch.
- Ask four questions for each flow: what data, why, who accesses, where stored.

### Slides 16-25 — 14-day DPDP Readiness Sprint
- Walk through data map, consent wording, privacy notice, WhatsApp/report handling, staff notes, journey testing and owner summary.
- Position ₹14,999 as practical readiness, not legal certification.

### Slides 26-32 — ROI math
- Frame ROI as avoided risk, patient trust, fewer lost enquiries, better public trust signals.
- Avoid fabricated numbers; mark insufficient data clearly.

### Slides 33-38 — Q&A prep
- Answer legal advice, WhatsApp, small-clinic scope, personal phones, cookie banners and first-fix prioritisation.

### Slides 39-45 — Founder Customer Program
- Explain early-stage offer honestly.
- No fake logos or invented case studies.
- Next steps: free Lost-Lead Audit, DPDP Sprint, contact details.
